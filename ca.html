Mains Booster

International Carbon Market Standards at CoP-29 (UNFCCC, Baku)

  • Carbon Markets: Allow governments/non-state actors to trade greenhouse gas credits.
  • Types: Compliance (government-managed) & Voluntary (independent organizations).
  • India: Framework for Compliance (Perform Achieve Trade) & Voluntary (Clean Development Mechanism).

RBI & SEBI Framework for FPI to FDI Reclassification

  • FPIs: Cap at 10% of an Indian company's paid-up equity; exceeding cap requires reclassification to FDI.
  • New framework guides reclassification process, as per Foreign Exchange Management Regulations, 2019.
  • Significance: Increases foreign investment flexibility, clarity for investors.

Non-Communicable Diseases (NCDs)

  • Chronic, long-duration diseases linked to urbanization, aging, unhealthy lifestyles.
  • India: National Programme for Prevention/Control of Cancer, Diabetes, CVD, and Stroke under NHM.

Global Carbon Budget Report (COP-29)

  • Published by Global Carbon Project (Estd. 2001), tracking carbon emissions trends and Paris Agreement goals.
  • Focus on CO₂, methane, nitrous oxide budgets.

India's Renewable Energy Sector (2023-2024)

  • Total capacity: 203.18 GW; non-fossil fuel capacity: 211.36 GW.
  • Solar: 92.12 GW, Wind: 47.72 GW, Hydro: 46.93 GW, Nuclear: 8.18 GW.

Adaptive Defence

  • Proactive mindset for anticipating threats, innovating, and adapting to unforeseen challenges.

Emerging Technologies in Warfare

  • Info warfare: Networked systems for informational advantage (e.g., Cyberwarfare).
  • Lethal Autonomous Weapons, Space-based Laser/Railgun tech, Synthetic Biology (gene-editing risks).

Nano-transporters

Mains Booster

Fake News

  • Fake news threatens citizens' Right to Information (RTI), upheld as a fundamental right by the Supreme Court in Raj Narayan vs. Uttar Pradesh Government (1975).
  • The Bombay High Court recently struck down the PIB Fact Check Unit, which flagged “fake news” about government activities, as it allowed the government to be the sole “arbiter” of truth regarding its own activities.

Anti-Fake News Initiatives

  • IT Rules 2021: Sets guidelines for content regulation by online news publishers and curated media.
  • Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita: Section 353 criminalizes spreading false information that causes public harm.
  • IT Act 2000: Section 66D punishes identity fraud through digital means.

Justice Sanjiv Khanna as New Chief Justice of India (CJI)

  • “First among equals”: In State of Rajasthan vs Prakash Chand (1997), the SC held the CJI as head of the judiciary without superior authority over other SC judges.
  • Judges are appointed by the President under Articles 124 and 217 (SC and HC).
  • Collegium System: CJI and four senior SC judges recommend appointments.

Anti-Drone Technologies

  • RF sensors detect drone signals across large areas.
  • High-Power Microwave (HPM) systems neutralize electronics and personnel over wide zones.
  • Integrated Drone Systems along the China border use jamming (“soft kills”) and lasers (“hard kills”).

Additional Anti-Drone Measures

  • Anti-Rogue Drone Technology Committee (ARDTC) evaluates technologies to counter rogue drones.
  • Drone Rules 2021: Divides airspace into Green, Yellow, and Red zones (no-fly).
  • Detailed vulnerability mapping along the Indo-Pak border.

World Intellectual Property Indicators 2024

  • India’s patent filings doubled from 2018 to 2023.
  • Globally, India ranks 6th in patent filings, 4th in trademarks, and 10th in industrial designs.

Supreme Court Affirms Accessibility for PwDs as a Fundamental Right:

  • The court outlined essential accessibility principles, such as universal design, inclusive solutions across disabilities, integration of assistive technologies, and consultation with relevant stakeholders.
  • A dual strategy was recommended: ensuring accessibility in current systems and transforming infrastructure for future projects.

Participatory Governance in India and Globally:

  • Land Acquisition Act, 2013: Mandates conducting a social impact assessment involving local communities.
  • Forest Rights Act, 2006: Recognizes the Gram Sabha as the statutory body, reflecting its participatory and democratic role.
  • Nagoya Protocol: Ensures that the benefits from using traditional knowledge are shared with indigenous populations.

India’s Efforts to Revamp Agrifood Systems:

  • Sustainable Agricultural Practices: Initiatives such as PKVY, PDMC, and the National Bamboo Mission promote eco-friendly farming methods.
  • Agricultural Infrastructure Development: The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF) and Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure (AMI) scheme are aimed at improving farming infrastructure.
  • Support for Farmers' Welfare: Programs like PM-KISAN and Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) are designed to boost farmers' income and welfare.

Current Demographic Trends in India:

  • Declining Fertility Rates: The national average is 2.0, with states like Tamil Nadu and Kerala at 1.4-1.5, and Bihar and Uttar Pradesh exceeding 2.5.
  • Ageing Population: The share of senior citizens is expected to rise to 15% by 2036, with Kerala's elderly population projected to reach 22.8%.
  • Old-Age Dependency Ratio: States like Kerala (26.1%) and Tamil Nadu (20.5%) face higher old-age dependency ratios compared to Bihar.

Concerns About the Declining Fertility Rate:

  • Ageing Societies: Increased demand for healthcare and pension services due to the growing elderly population.
  • Rising Dependency: Higher dependency ratios place an economic and emotional burden on the younger workforce.
  • Political Impact: Slower-growing states may lose political representation when the freeze on Parliament seats expires in 2026.
  • Labour Shortages: Reduced fertility rates may lead to workforce shortages, as seen in countries like Japan.

Comics Commandos Initiative:

  • In Assam’s Goalpara district, the "Comics Commandos" initiative was launched to combat child labour and child marriage.
