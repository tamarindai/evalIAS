Loss of Groundwater Shifting Earth's Axis

  • Key Findings: Excessive groundwater extraction shifted Earth's rotational pole by 80 cm between 1993–2010, causing a 0.24-inch rise in sea levels.
  • Mechanism: Redistribution of Earth's mass due to aquifer depletion led to these changes.
  • Implications: Highlights the impact of human activities on Earth's physical systems and challenges existing climate models.

Key Changes in the RBI’s Currency Policy

  • Since late 2022, the RBI has actively intervened in the forex market, reducing rupee-dollar volatility to 1.9% (April 2023–August 2024) from the previous average of 5% (1991–2020).
  • The RBI’s direct actions, rather than free-market forces, now primarily stabilize the rupee’s exchange rate, resembling a pegged system and raising concerns over export competitiveness and transparency.
  • Pegging distorts market signals, potentially makes exports costlier, and risks market trust, contrasting with the benefits of India’s previous flexible exchange rate policy that promoted stability and competitiveness naturally.

Impact of Trump’s Presidency on Global Agreements and India's Adaptation

  • Trump’s administration withdrew the US from major multilateral agreements (Paris Agreement, TPP, WHO) and strained global trade frameworks (WTO, US-China trade war), challenging norms and weakening multilateralism.
  • His “America First” approach prioritized US national interests, reducing US leadership in global governance and shifting focus from multilateral to bilateral or regional agreements.
  • In response, India has adapted by strengthening bilateral trade, joining smaller coalitions (e.g., Quad, Artemis Accords), and investing in independent energy and tech solutions to counterbalance the decline in multilateral cooperation.
