  • Forest Rights Act, 2006: Recognizes the Gram Sabha as the statutory body, reflecting its participatory and democratic role.
  • Nagoya Protocol: Ensures that the benefits from using traditional knowledge are shared with indigenous populations.

Current Demographic Trends in India:

  • Declining Fertility Rates: The national average is 2.0, with states like Tamil Nadu and Kerala at 1.4-1.5, and Bihar and Uttar Pradesh exceeding 2.5.
  • Ageing Population: The share of senior citizens is expected to rise to 15% by 2036, with Kerala's elderly population projected to reach 22.8%.
  • Old-Age Dependency Ratio: States like Kerala (26.1%) and Tamil Nadu (20.5%) face higher old-age dependency ratios compared to Bihar.

Concerns About the Declining Fertility Rate:

  • Ageing Societies: Increased demand for healthcare and pension services due to the growing elderly population.
  • Rising Dependency: Higher dependency ratios place an economic and emotional burden on the younger workforce.
  • Political Impact: Slower-growing states may lose political representation when the freeze on Parliament seats expires in 2026.
  • Labour Shortages: Reduced fertility rates may lead to workforce shortages, as seen in countries like Japan.
