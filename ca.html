Mains Booster

Tsunami

  • Definition: Series of massive waves caused by underwater disturbances like earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and landslides.
  • Steps Taken by India:
    • ITEWC: Provides tsunami advisories to 25 Indian Ocean countries.
    • Tsunami Buoy System: Real-time data transmission network.
    • INCOIS Tide Gauges: Real-time monitoring along the coast.
    • NDMA Guidelines: Comprehensive management framework.
    • Modeling and Mapping: Early warning for tsunamis and storm surges.

Climate Finance Mechanisms

  • Global Environment Facility (1991): Tackles global environmental issues, including climate change.
  • Green Climate Fund (2010): Supports developing nations; established at COP 16.
  • NCQG: Future financial target post-2025 for climate action.

Terrorism and Organized Crime Linkages

  • Financing: Organized crimes fund terrorism (e.g., drug trafficking by Northeast militants).
  • Logistics: Organized groups transport terrorists and return foreign fighters (e.g., Moreh and Cox’s Bazaar in the Chittagong Hill Tracts).
  • Counterfeit Currency: Terrorists spread fake Indian currency (e.g., in Kashmir).

Countermeasures

  • UNTOC (2000): Key instrument against transnational crime.
  • FATF: Sets global standards to combat money laundering and terror financing.
  • INTERPOL: Coordinates efforts against international crime (196 members).
  • UNODC: Fights organized crime, corruption, terrorism, and illicit drugs.

Direct-to-Device Satellite Technology

  • Principle: Satellites replace terrestrial cell towers for mobile connectivity.
  • NTN Technology: Enables direct communication between devices and satellites.
  • BSNL: Uses Viasat’s Geostationary L-band satellites for remote coverage.

Guru Nanak Dev Ji’s Teachings

  • Ik Onkar: Belief in one universal God.
  • Truthfulness: Advocated truth as life’s highest virtue.
  • Social Equality: Opposed caste-based and social discrimination.
  • Religion: Warned against using it for material gain.

Modern Relevance

  • Equality: Addresses gender/caste discrimination and global conflicts.
  • Vaand Chhako: Reduces inequality.
  • Kirat Karo: Promotes honesty, reducing corruption.
  • Sarbat da Bhala: Advocates for ecological justice.

State Finance Commissions (SFCs)

  • Constitution: Article 243I mandates SFC formation post-73rd Amendment.
  • Tenure: Every 5 years.
  • Mandate: Review Panchayat/Municipality finances, recommend tax distribution, and grants.

Ministry of Home Affairs Reimposed AFSPA in Violence-Hit Areas of Manipur

SC Guidelines on Demolition of Property

  • Demolition without due process violates natural justice, due process, and the rule of law.
  • Demolitions infringe on fundamental rights, like the Right to Shelter under Article 21.
  • A 15-day notice is mandatory for unauthorized construction.

Anti-Trafficking Steps in India

  • Legal Framework: Prajwala vs. Union case, ITPA 1956, Article 23.
  • Administrative Measures: MHA anti-trafficking units, MoUs with countries.
  • International Obligations: Ratified UNCTOC; BNS includes trafficking offenses.

Key Changes in the RBI’s Currency Policy

  • Since late 2022, the RBI has actively intervened in the forex market, reducing rupee-dollar volatility to 1.9% (April 2023–August 2024) from the previous average of 5% (1991–2020).
  • The RBI’s direct actions, rather than free-market forces, now primarily stabilize the rupee’s exchange rate, resembling a pegged system and raising concerns over export competitiveness and transparency.
  • Pegging distorts market signals, potentially makes exports costlier, and risks market trust, contrasting with the benefits of India’s previous flexible exchange rate policy that promoted stability and competitiveness naturally.

Impact of Trump’s Presidency on Global Agreements and India's Adaptation

  • Trump’s administration withdrew the US from major multilateral agreements (Paris Agreement, TPP, WHO) and strained global trade frameworks (WTO, US-China trade war), challenging norms and weakening multilateralism.
  • His “America First” approach prioritized US national interests, reducing US leadership in global governance and shifting focus from multilateral to bilateral or regional agreements.
  • In response, India has adapted by strengthening bilateral trade, joining smaller coalitions (e.g., Quad, Artemis Accords), and investing in independent energy and tech solutions to counterbalance the decline in multilateral cooperation.

Fake News

  • Fake news threatens citizens' Right to Information (RTI), upheld as a fundamental right by the Supreme Court in Raj Narayan vs. Uttar Pradesh Government (1975).
  • The Bombay High Court recently struck down the PIB Fact Check Unit, which flagged “fake news” about government activities, as it allowed the government to be the sole “arbiter” of truth regarding its own activities.

Anti-Fake News Initiatives

  • IT Rules 2021: Sets guidelines for content regulation by online news publishers and curated media.
  • Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita: Section 353 criminalizes spreading false information that causes public harm.
  • IT Act 2000: Section 66D punishes identity fraud through digital means.

Justice Sanjiv Khanna as New Chief Justice of India (CJI)

  • “First among equals”: In State of Rajasthan vs Prakash Chand (1997), the SC held the CJI as head of the judiciary without superior authority over other SC judges.
  • Judges are appointed by the President under Articles 124 and 217 (SC and HC).
  • Collegium System: CJI and four senior SC judges recommend appointments.

Supreme Court Affirms Accessibility for PwDs as a Fundamental Right:

  • The court outlined essential accessibility principles, such as universal design, inclusive solutions across disabilities, integration of assistive technologies, and consultation with relevant stakeholders.
  • A dual strategy was recommended: ensuring accessibility in current systems and transforming infrastructure for future projects.

Participatory Governance in India and Globally:

  • Land Acquisition Act, 2013: Mandates conducting a social impact assessment involving local communities.
  • Forest Rights Act, 2006: Recognizes the Gram Sabha as the statutory body, reflecting its participatory and democratic role.
  • Nagoya Protocol: Ensures that the benefits from using traditional knowledge are shared with indigenous populations.

India’s Efforts to Revamp Agrifood Systems:

  • Sustainable Agricultural Practices: Initiatives such as PKVY, PDMC, and the National Bamboo Mission promote eco-friendly farming methods.
  • Agricultural Infrastructure Development: The Agriculture Infrastructure Fund (AIF) and Agricultural Marketing Infrastructure (AMI) scheme are aimed at improving farming infrastructure.
  • Support for Farmers' Welfare: Programs like PM-KISAN and Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) are designed to boost farmers' income and welfare.

Current Demographic Trends in India:

  • Declining Fertility Rates: The national average is 2.0, with states like Tamil Nadu and Kerala at 1.4-1.5, and Bihar and Uttar Pradesh exceeding 2.5.
  • Ageing Population: The share of senior citizens is expected to rise to 15% by 2036, with Kerala's elderly population projected to reach 22.8%.
  • Old-Age Dependency Ratio: States like Kerala (26.1%) and Tamil Nadu (20.5%) face higher old-age dependency ratios compared to Bihar.

Concerns About the Declining Fertility Rate:

  • Ageing Societies: Increased demand for healthcare and pension services due to the growing elderly population.
  • Rising Dependency: Higher dependency ratios place an economic and emotional burden on the younger workforce.
  • Political Impact: Slower-growing states may lose political representation when the freeze on Parliament seats expires in 2026.
  • Labour Shortages: Reduced fertility rates may lead to workforce shortages, as seen in countries like Japan.
